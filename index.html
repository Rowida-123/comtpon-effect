Evidence: Photon is a Particle 5 key proofs · Compton (1923)
01 · momentum

Definite Momentum Transfer

The recoiling electron carries a precise, calculable momentum — exactly as in a billiard-ball collision. Waves cannot deliver a fixed quantum of momentum to a single particle.

02 · conservation

Exact Conservation Laws

Δλ = (h/m₀c)(1−cosθ) is derived purely from conservation of energy and momentum treating the photon as a particle. Matches experiment to 5 significant figures.

03 · discrete peaks

Two Sharp Spectral Lines

Both the original λ₀ and shifted λ' appear as sharp lines — not a continuous smear. Only particle-like one-to-one collisions produce sharp discrete peaks.

04 · angle shift

Angle-Dependent Shift

Δλ depends only on θ, not incident wavelength. Classical Thomson scattering predicts zero change at all angles — completely contradicted by experiment.

05 · mass scaling

Mass-Dependent Shift

Switch to proton target — the shift nearly vanishes because λ_c = h/mc scales with mass. Confirms the photon collides with a single definite particle.
